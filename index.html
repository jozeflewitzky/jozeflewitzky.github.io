How Important Is Your CSA Safety Score?

CSA safety scores for fleet managers and commercial drivers. Technical without being impenetrable.

There are over 5 million trucks on the road in the United States of America. To keep track of safety and compliance, the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) created inspection guidelines for reducing accidents to keep everyone on the roads as safe as possible. They have mainly accomplished this through the Compliance, Safety, Accountability (CSA) program. The program uses its CSA Safety Score to help identify and track safety problems with carrier fleets. As a truck driver or owner of a trucking company, understanding this safety score is crucial to ensuring a successful business. What Is the CSA Safety Score? The FMCSA designates the CSA Safety Score to hold owner-operators accountable for road safety. The score is a percentile rank from 0 to 100, with 0 indicating the safest drivers and 100 the worst safety violators. The CSA Safety Score is tallied per company, not per vehicle. The FMCSA tallies the scores through roadside inspections updated and stored monthly in their Safety Measurement System (SMS). The data tally has seven categories, called the Behavior Analysis and Safety Improvement Categories (BASICs): driver fitness, crash indicator, hours of service (HOS) compliance, hazardous materials compliance, controlled substances and alcohol, vehicle maintenance, and unsafe driving.
